Bio

The Spanish defender joined from Anderlecht in August 2022, with the left-back having made 49 appearances for the Belgian club across the 2021/22 campaign.

Sergio began his career as part of Barcelona’s youth set-up before leaving the Catalan club to join Bundesliga side Borussia Dortmund at the start of 2018.

He then subsequently spent two seasons back in Spain on loan at Huesca helping the club earn promotion to La Liga at the end of the 2019/20 campaign before then joining Anderlecht in the summer of 2021.

Gomez went on to enjoy a fine individual campaign under the guidance of former Anderlecht boss Vincent Kompany and was subsequently voted as the Belgian club’s Player of the Year.

He has featured occasionally in the senior side through the first months of the his debut season with City and looks an impressive addition to the squad.

Gomez has gained invaluable experience in the role he played in City securing a third consecutive Premier League title.

And the Spain international will be keen to get more game time in the 2023/24 campaign as City will look to retain three trophies having won the FA Cup and the Club’s first ever Champions League trophy to top off a thrilling debut season for the defender.

Gomez was joint-top goal-scorer in the Under-21 Euros finals. He played in all but one game as Spain finished second, losing 1-0 in the final to England.

The Spaniard added his fourth accolade to his City career as he was part of the squad who beat Sevilla on penalties to lift the UEFA Super Cup for the first time in the Club’s history in August 2023. as well as the FIFA Club World Cup in December the same year.
